The Big Bet

The AlphaFold Moment for Quantum Sensing

Atom interferometers are among the most promising technologies for GPS-denied navigation, gravimetry, and precision sensing. Yet their development remains constrained by slow, expensive, and fragile physical experimentation.

QuantumLeap makes a different bet: that validated, continuously learning AI can replace most early-stage hardware iteration—without sacrificing scientific rigor or mission trust.

60% Faster Validation Cycles

Collapse concept-to-prototype timelines by replacing months of physical testing with validated prediction.

1000× Design Iteration

Explore orders of magnitude more configurations than any laboratory could physically test.

Democratized Access

Bring advanced quantum sensor design within reach of students, researchers, and partners worldwide.

Core Innovations

Real-to-Sim Collaborative Learning

QuantumLeap continuously improves by incorporating real experimental data from distributed institutions worldwide. Each validated contribution refines model accuracy for the entire community, creating compounding network effects.

  • Attribution and academic credit

Validated Digital Twin as Hardware Replacement

QuantumLeap is designed to substitute for physical atom interferometer hardware in design exploration, mission planning, algorithm development, and training—within rigorously defined accuracy envelopes.

  • <5% MAE target for certified use cases
  • Mission planning and Monte Carlo analysis
  • Virtual sensor output streams
  • Qualification and renewal framework

Validation

Validation Is the Product

QuantumLeap is built around a formal validation framework that defines where and how the digital twin can be trusted. Accuracy targets, benchmark systems, and certification pathways are explicit, measured, and continuously audited.

  • Accuracy thresholds by application domain
  • Cross-laboratory validation (3+ institutions)
  • Benchmark systems including NPS, JILA, and MIT Lincoln Lab
  • Full provenance tracking for every model version

"Every prediction is traceable—to data, contributors, and measured accuracy."
